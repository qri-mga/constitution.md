## Preamble

Qualia is a Middle Georgia State University (MGA) student organization dedicated to responsible artificial intelligence (AI) education, research, and civic engagement. We treat AI like science: we value evidence, reproducibility, transparency, and honest uncertainty, and we keep humans at the center of our work.

This Constitution establishes the minimum rules for governance, membership, and operations for Qualia as a Recognized Student Organization (RSO).

## Article I — Name and purpose

### A. Name and purpose

The name of this student organization shall be Qualia (the “Organization”) and the purpose of this organization shall be to build an inclusive, human-first community for AI learning and responsible innovation at MGA. Qualia will:

- Provide AI literacy through workshops, reading groups, and hands-on demonstrations that encourage curiosity and critical thinking.
- Support student and faculty collaboration on ethical, safety-minded AI projects, emphasizing reproducible methods and transparent reporting.
- Create spaces for respectful debate about the societal impacts of AI and to help the campus community adapt safely to increasing AI integration.
- Encourage service and outreach that helps the public understand AI benefits, risks, and responsible use.

### B. University affiliation

This organization will support the mission of the Office of Student Engagement and Middle Georgia State University and will subscribe to the policies and procedures of the University System of Georgia and Middle Georgia State University.

## Article II — Membership

### A. Eligibility

Active membership is confined to regularly enrolled Middle Georgia State University students who have a minimum academic load of three (3) hours during each semester of participation.

### B. Non-discrimination

Membership and all privileges, including voting and officer positions, are extended to all eligible students and will not be denied on the basis of race, national origin, color, sex, age, religion, or disability. Qualia also affirms that membership is open without regard to ethnicity, gender, gender identity or expression, sexual orientation, or veteran status, consistent with MGA policy.

### C. Member expectations

Members are expected to uphold MGA standards of academic integrity and civility, to participate respectfully in discussions, and to follow all University and USG policies.

### D. Active member and voting status

An “Active Member” is a member who (1) attends at least half of regular meetings or events within a semester and (2) contributes to at least one Organization activity (e.g., a project team, event support, outreach, or documentation). Active Members in good standing may vote in elections and constitutional amendments.

## Article III — Officers

### A. Executive board

Officers of this organization will be:

- President
- Vice President
- Secretary
- Treasurer

### B. Duties of officers

- **President:** Provides overall leadership; chairs meetings; serves as the primary liaison to the Office of Student Life/Engagement and advisors; ensures policy compliance; oversees committees and projects; represents Qualia in official communications.
- **Vice President:** Coordinates operations, recruitment, and outreach; manages logistics for meetings and events; supports committee leads; assumes President duties when necessary.
- **Secretary:** Maintains meeting agendas and minutes; manages official records (membership roster, constitution/bylaws, approvals); coordinates announcements and communications.
- **Treasurer:** Maintains financial records; prepares budgets; manages funding requests and reimbursements; ensures all funds are handled through the MGA agency account and according to MGA/USG requirements.

### C. Officer eligibility

All students holding an elected or appointed position of leadership and acting as an officer of this organization must maintain at least a 2.0 cumulative grade point average (GPA), be in good standing, and must be enrolled at Middle Georgia State University during the time of service.

### D. Terms and elections

Officers serve one-year terms. Elections will be held toward the end of the Spring semester. Nominations may be accepted from the floor or submitted in writing. Officers shall be elected by a simple majority vote of Active Members in good standing. If an election cannot be held, the Executive Board may appoint an interim officer until an election can occur.

## Article IV — Meetings

### A. Frequency

This organization shall meet regularly during the fall and spring semesters. Meetings during the summer semester will be held as needed.

### B. Scheduling

All meetings, including the dates, times, and locations will be confirmed with the Office of Student Engagement prior to publication and/or announcement of these meetings.

### C. Quorum and voting

A quorum for voting is defined as at least 40% of Active Members. Unless otherwise specified in this Constitution, motions pass by simple majority of those present and voting.

## Article V — Faculty/Staff advisor

### A. Requirement

All recognized student organizations at Middle Georgia State University are required to have a faculty or staff advisor. While the advisor should be selected by student members, all RSO advisors must also seek the approval of their division chair and/or supervisor.

### B. Responsibilities

The advisor of record accepts the responsibilities of this position and will approve all events and activities of this organization, providing guidance on policy compliance, risk management, and ethical considerations.

### C. Advisors of Qualia

Qualia will maintain at least one active advisor. The Organization may have multiple advisors; one will be designated as the primary contact for official communications.

## Article VI — Revenue

### A. Deposits

All monies collected from membership dues and from all fundraisers must be deposited into the organization’s Agency Account. These deposits will be made through the Bursar’s Office as soon as possible after the monies have been collected.

### B. No petty cash

No petty cash or slush funds will be kept by any member or advisor of this organization.

### C. Fundraiser approval

All fundraisers will be approved by the Office of Student Engagement at least two weeks prior to the actual event and will follow all of the guidelines as outlined in the RSO Handbook.

### D. Relationship to external organizations

Qualia is a student organization of MGA and is distinct from any external nonprofit or business. Any collaboration with external entities must be transparent, approved through MGA procedures when required, and must never involve commingling of funds or misuse of MGA names, marks, or resources.

## Article VII — Ratification

### A.

This constitution is subject to the approval by the Student Government Association and the Office of Student Engagement.

### B.

Following the approval of SGA and Student Engagement, the founding members of the RSO and advisor will be notified by the Office of Student Engagement.

## Article VIII — Bylaws

### A.

This constitution may be supplemented with bylaws which pertain to the above articles.

### B.

An electronic copy of any and all bylaws will be filed with the Office of Student Engagement.

## Article IX — Amendments/Supplements

### A.

A written notice will be presented to the membership of this organization at a regular meeting to amend or supplement any part of this constitution. The proposed amendment/supplement will be voted upon no sooner than the next regularly scheduled meeting and must receive a two-thirds (2/3) majority vote of all Active Members in good standing.

### B.

Any amendments/supplements to this constitution will then be submitted to the Office of Student Engagement for approval.

## Article X — Dissolution

In the event of dissolution, all remaining organizational funds will be handled according to MGA policy. Unless otherwise directed by MGA, remaining funds shall be transferred to the Middle Georgia State University Foundation for purposes that support student learning and research initiatives.

## Bylaws (Operating rules)

These bylaws provide operational details consistent with the Constitution. If any bylaw conflicts with the Constitution or MGA/USG policy, the Constitution and applicable policies control.

### Bylaw 1 — Values and code of conduct

Qualia is committed to evidence-first learning, respectful debate, accessibility, and ethical behavior.

- No harassment, hazing, or discrimination. Members must follow MGA conduct expectations.
- No illegal activity. Qualia does not sponsor or endorse hacking, unauthorized access, malware, or policy-violating conduct.
- Academic integrity is mandatory: no plagiarism, cheating, or misrepresentation of results.
- Safety mindset: projects should be fail-closed where feasible and should avoid foreseeable harm.

### Bylaw 2 — Committees

The Executive Board may charter committees to distribute leadership and give members meaningful ownership. Suggested standing committees:

- **Education & Literacy:** workshops, reading groups, beginner-friendly onboarding.
- **Research & Projects:** project proposals, reproducibility practices, demos, internal reviews.
- **Community & Outreach:** tabling, guest speakers, partnerships, campus communications.
- **Operations:** room requests, accessibility planning, documentation, meeting logistics.

### Bylaw 3 — Project approval and oversight

To run a project under Qualia’s name or to use Qualia resources, a team must submit a project proposal to the Executive Board.

- The proposal must state goals, methods, expected outputs, and ethical or safety considerations.
- Projects involving sensitive data, human subjects, or elevated risk require advisor review and any required MGA approvals before work begins.
- All project teams must keep a simple public record (e.g., a shared document or repository) with meeting notes and reproducibility details when appropriate.
- The Executive Board may pause or terminate any project that violates policy or creates substantial risk.

### Bylaw 4 — Events and accessibility

All events must be planned with accessibility in mind (physical access, alternative formats for materials, and inclusive communication). Event dates, times, and locations must be approved as required by MGA procedures.

- No alcohol purchases using student activity funds and compliance with MGA alcohol and drug-free policies.
- Guest speakers and co-hosted events must be coordinated through the advisors and any required campus offices.
- Events should include a short safety and conduct reminder at the start.

### Bylaw 5 — Communications and data privacy

Qualia will minimize personal data collection and will protect member information.

- Official communications should use MGA email addresses when possible.
- Member rosters and contact lists are for internal use only and should not be shared publicly without consent.
- Any online platforms used (e.g., Discord, Slack) must have clear moderation rules and reporting pathways.

### Bylaw 6 — Financial practices

The Treasurer will keep transparent records and provide a brief financial update at least once per semester.

- All funds must flow through MGA’s agency account and MGA-approved processes.
- Purchases and reimbursements require the approvals and documentation required by MGA.
- Fundraising must be approved per MGA timelines and rules.

### Bylaw 7 — Conflicts of interest and external relationships

Officers and members must disclose conflicts of interest when a decision may benefit an external organization or personal project.

- When collaborating with external organizations, Qualia will not misrepresent affiliation or endorse products/services on behalf of MGA.
- Any use of Qualia branding in external contexts must be approved by the Executive Board and, when required, by MGA.

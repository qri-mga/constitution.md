## Article I — Name and purpose

### Section 1. Name

The name of this student organization shall be **Qualia**, **QRI**, or **Q\***.

### Section 2. Purpose

The purpose of Q\* is to advance interdisciplinary research into the theoretical and applied foundations of **Artificial General Intelligence (AGI)**. The organization promotes inquiry at the intersection of **cognitive architecture**, **AI alignment**, **interpretability**, and **computational theories of consciousness**, with an emphasis on ethical development, reproducible methods, and intellectual collaboration.

Q\* will:

1. Foster an environment for student and faculty collaboration on original AI research.
2. Encourage academic publication, experimentation, and public engagement in emerging AI systems.
3. Develop student competencies in data science, cognitive systems, and computational modeling.
4. Contribute to MGA’s academic reputation through research, mentorship, and scholarly discourse.

### Section 3. University affiliation

This organization supports the mission of Middle Georgia State University and subscribes to the policies of the **Office of Student Engagement**, the **School of Computing**, and the **University System of Georgia**.

## Article II — Membership

### Section 1. Eligibility

Active membership shall be open to all regularly enrolled Middle Georgia State University students carrying a minimum academic load of **three (3) credit hours** per semester, who demonstrate interest in artificial intelligence, cognitive science, or related fields.

Membership shall not be denied based on **race, national origin, color, sex, age, religion, disability, gender identity, or creed**.

### Section 2. Expectations

Members are expected to:

- Participate in at least one research or technical discussion each semester.
- Uphold MGA’s standards of academic integrity.
- Contribute respectfully to a collaborative and inclusive research environment.

### Section 3. Active membership

Active members are defined as those who attend at least **50%** of meetings or events within a semester and participate in at least one project or initiative.

## Article III — Officers

### Section 1. Executive board

Officers of QRI shall consist of:

- **President** (Research Director)
- **Vice President** (Operations and Outreach)
- **Secretary** (Documentation and Communication)
- **Treasurer** (Finance and Resources)

### Section 2. Duties

**A. President (Research Director)**

- Provides overall leadership, vision, and coordination of research and administrative activities.
- Serves as the primary liaison between Q\*, the Faculty Advisor, and the Office of Student Engagement.
- Oversees meetings, project approvals, and external representation.

**B. Vice President (Operations and Outreach)**

- Coordinates internal operations, project logistics, and recruitment efforts.
- Oversees the onboarding of new members and collaborative partnerships.

**C. Secretary (Documentation and Communication)**

- Maintains minutes, records, and archives of meetings and projects.
- Manages digital communication, including announcements and website updates.

**D. Treasurer (Finance and Resources)**

- Maintains financial transparency and records of all transactions.
- Manages budgets, funding applications, and fundraising compliance.

### Section 3. Officer requirements

All officers must:

- Maintain a **minimum cumulative GPA of 2.5**.
- Be enrolled at Middle Georgia State University during their term of office.
- Uphold the ethical and academic values of the organization.

### Section 4. Elections

Elections will occur during the **Spring semester**. Nominations shall be accepted from the floor, and officers shall be elected by a **majority vote** of members in good standing. Outgoing officers will assist in the transition of leadership.

## Article IV — Meetings

### Section 1. Frequency

The organization shall meet **biweekly** during the fall and spring semesters. Summer meetings may be held as needed.

### Section 2. Administration

Meeting schedules, locations, and event dates must be confirmed with the **Office of Student Engagement** prior to publication.

### Section 3. Attendance

Members are encouraged to maintain consistent attendance. Repeated unexcused absences may result in loss of active membership status or voting privileges, as determined by the Executive Board.

## Article V — Faculty/Staff advisors

### Section 1. Appointment

All recognized student organizations at MGA are required to maintain a **faculty or staff advisor**. The advisor shall be selected by student members and approved by their division chair or supervisor.

### Section 2. Responsibilities

The Faculty Advisor shall:

- Provide academic oversight and ethical guidance on research activities.
- Approve all events, projects, and official communications.
- Facilitate connections between Q\* and institutional resources or external partners.

## Article VI — Revenue

### Section 1. Funds and deposits

All funds generated through fundraising, donations, or optional dues shall be deposited into the organization’s **Agency Account** through the MGA **Bursar’s Office** promptly after collection.

### Section 2. Financial conduct

No petty cash or unreported funds shall be kept by any member or advisor. All expenditures must be approved by the Treasurer and Faculty Advisor.

### Section 3. Fundraising approval

All fundraising events must be approved by the **Office of Student Engagement** at least **two (2) weeks** before the event and comply with the MGA RSO Handbook.

## Article VII — Ratification

This constitution is subject to review and approval by the **Student Government Association (SGA)** and the **Office of Student Engagement**. Following approval, Q\* shall be formally recognized as a Registered Student Organization of Middle Georgia State University.

## Article VIII — Bylaws

### Section 1. Development

This constitution may be supplemented by bylaws defining operational details such as membership tiers, committees, or project approval processes.

### Section 2. Filing

An electronic copy of any bylaws shall be submitted to the **Office of Student Engagement** and updated annually.

## Article IX — Amendments

### Section 1. Proposal and vote

Amendments to this constitution must be presented in writing during a regular meeting and may not be voted upon until the following meeting. A **two-thirds (2/3) majority** of active members in good standing is required for adoption.

### Section 2. Approval

Any adopted amendment must be submitted to the **Office of Student Engagement** for administrative approval before taking effect.

## Article X — Dissolution

In the event of dissolution, all remaining organizational funds shall be transferred to the **Middle Georgia State University Foundation** under a designation supporting student research initiatives.

*Ratified this **8th** day of **November**, **2025** by the founding members of Qualia (Q\*) at Middle Georgia State University.*
